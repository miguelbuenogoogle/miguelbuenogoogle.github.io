As a past beneficiary of federally funded programs like the Educational Opportunity Program (EOP) and TRIO's Ronald E. McNair Postbaccalaureate Achievement Program –and a former tutor for TRIO's Upward Bound and Project Rebound– I deeply value community and educational access for underrepresented students.

I'm an active alumnus remaining deeply engaged in academic and professional mentorship. In 2024 and 2026 I served as a judge for Fresno State's DataFest, a data analysis competition hosted by the American Statistical Association (ASA), and in 2025, served as its keynote speaker.

I also manage the Fresno State Economics Alumni and Friends LinkedIn group, which I founded in 2019. With over 140 members, the group fosters collegiality among Fresno State Economics alumni, students, and the community while also serving as a liaison between the Department and its alumni. Through this platform, I coordinate the annual Econ Alumni Working In Panel Series, featuring professionals from organizations like Google, Apple, and the Federal Reserve.

As part of my commitment to expanding access and sharing institutional knowledge, I have hosted several academic groups at Google, including the Fresno State Mathematics, Computer Science, and Economics Departments, the Career Center, and UC Santa Cruz's Economics Department.

Through these commitments, I aim to build a more equitable world where people from underrepresented communities can meaningfully support their families and have the freedom to pursue their intellectual curiosity.
